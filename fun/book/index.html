Books

Reading books has always been an important part of my life. Despite having poor memory and constantly forget the details of many books that I have read, I enjoy acquiring knowledge and cultivating sentiments by reading. Most of the books I read are in Chinese, some in English. Since I was young, I liked going to bookstores and libraries to read or just to wander around. After graduation，I still go to the university library constantly whenever I feel I need a change of atmosphere, even though I mostly read e-books nowadays.

I usually adopt Syntopical Reading, which involves reading many books on the same subject and comparing and contrasting the ideas, vocabulary, and arguments. The topics of my reading could be quite broad. Recently, My interest lies in The History of Isreal and the Jewish People. Check out what I am reading now at GoodReads.

Books that I like

Here are a few books that I liked, listed in no particular order. Technical books and books on art or music are not included.

Fiction

  • Three Kings, by Ah Cheng（阿城). This book, which consists of three novels, The King of Chess (Chinese: 棋王, "Qi Wang"), The King of Trees (Chinese: 树王, "Shu Wang") and The King of Children (Chinese: 孩子王, "Haizi Wang"), is a consummate work of Chinese characters. I like the spare, concentrated expressiveness of his prose style.
  • Jean-Christophe, by Romain Rolland. The novel’s protagonist, Jean-Christophe Krafft, is a composer of German birth—modeled in part on Ludwig van Beethoven and in part on Rolland himself—who, despite discouragement and the stresses of his own turbulent personality, is inspired by love of life.
  • The Count of Monte Cristo (French: Le Comte de Monte-Cristo), by Alexandre Dumas.
  • The Baron in the Trees, by Archibald Colquhoun.
  • The Glass Bead Game, Siddhartha and Narcissus and Goldmund, by Hermann Hesse.
  • The Decay of the Angel, by Yukio Mishima (みしま ゆきお). The dramatic climax of the SEA OF FERTILITY, bringing together the dominant themes of the three previous novels; the decay of Japan's courtly tradition and samurai ideal, and the essence and value of Buddhist philosophy.

Prose and Poetry

  • Zhuangzi, by Zhuangzi (Chuang-tzu 莊子 “Master Zhuang”). It is the pivotal figure in Classical Philosophical Daoism written in late 4th century BC.
  • The poems of Su Dong-Po, by Su Dong-Po.
  • The Book of Disquiet, by Fernando Pessoa.
  • Ocnos, by Luis Cernuda.
  • Twenty Love Poems and a Song of Despair, by Pablo Neruda.
  • Sonnets to Orpheus, by Rainer Maria Rilke.
